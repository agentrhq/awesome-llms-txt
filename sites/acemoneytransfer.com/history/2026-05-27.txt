## Frequently Asked Questions


## Check out the queries below for more information.


### How do I send money online?


Sending money online with ACE money transfer is as easy as it gets. All you need to do is:


1. Create an account using our website or mobile app.
2. Complete your profile with basic details and required documents.
3. Choose the destination country, and desired payout option, and enter the receiver’s details.
4. Pay for the transaction using your Debit/Credit Card or your Bank Account.


Do you want to see how these steps work? Click the button below:


[View Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntezOm25bZI)


### What are Sending Methods?


You can pay for your online transactions using:


1. Debit Card
2. Credit Card
3. Online Bank Transfer via Trsutly (Except UK)
4. Ecospend Only from the UK for online bank transfer method.
5. Google Pay
6. Apple Pay
7. Bancontact (Belgium only)
8. iDeal (Netherlands only)


### How can I create a transaction?


To place the transaction, you need to log in to your account. Once the dashboard opens, follow these simple steps:


1. Click on "Send Money".
2. Choose the Destination Country.
3. Select the payout option, i.e., Cash Pick-up or Bank Deposit.
4. Select the payout location.
5. Enter the amount.
6. Create or choose the Beneficiary.
7. Confirm Bank details (For bank deposit only).

### How are customer's funds safeguarded?


### Customer Fund Safeguarding Practices


- **Segregation of Funds:** We maintain a clear separation between customer funds and the company's operational or own funds. Customer funds are placed in designated accounts that are separate from our general business accounts until the customer's transaction has been successfully delivered to the intended beneficiary.
- **Designated Accounts:** Customer funds are held in designated bank accounts with reputable financial institutions. These banks are chosen based on their stability and their compliance with local and global regulations.
- **Independent Audits:** Regular, independent audits and reconciliations are conducted to ensure that customer funds are correctly segregated and that the firm is in compliance with the regulations. These audits provide additional layers of oversight and verification.
