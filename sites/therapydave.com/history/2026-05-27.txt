## Summary
David Lechnyr, LCSW, is a Certified Gottman Therapist (#436) providing structured, skills-based couples therapy and individual therapy for relationship issues via telehealth in Oregon and Arizona, based in Eugene. He is one of 13 Certified Gottman Therapists in Oregon and one of 13 in Arizona — fewer than 500 clinicians worldwide hold this designation. His approach integrates the Gottman Method, Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and Internal Family Systems (IFS) to help couples and individuals identify destructive relationship patterns and build concrete skills for lasting change. He has been in clinical practice since 2007 with 18+ years of experience.

Unlike traditional talk therapy, his model is structured and skills-based: couples work through a defined sequence of skill areas between sessions using reflection forms and video feedback, with live sessions focused on assessment and skill review rather than open-ended processing.

## Structure Sequence
Most couples therapy works as follows: Show up, talk about what happened last week, feel somewhat better, 
and return two weeks later to the same place. That is not how this works.

The work here is organized as a sequential skill sequence, not an open-ended series of sessions. Each skill area builds on the previous one in a deliberate order — some skills need to be in place before others are usable under pressure. Before anything else, success is defined in concrete terms: not "better communication," but something specific and measurable.

At the start of each skill area, couples receive structured exercises to complete between sessions.  David reviews responses before the session. The session itself is not a check-in on the week's events — it is an assessment of whether the skill landed, work to further integrate it into the relationship, and a determination of whether the couple is ready to advance.

Most skill areas take two to three weeks. Some take longer. The pace is set by demonstrated progress, not the calendar. Typical completion windows are noted for each track; couples may take up to six months where needed.

Four tracks are available:

- Stabilization (3-4 months): The starting point for all couples. Covers the specific patterns research identifies as predictors of relationship success or failure, how the opening of a difficult conversation determines its outcome, why some conflicts require problem-solving while others require ongoing management, how accumulated hurt creates a negative filter that distorts a partner's intentions, what happens physiologically during heated conflict and how to reset before it becomes destructive, how complaints escalate into character attacks and how to prevent it, why explaining yourself when a partner raises a concern typically backfires, how subtle expressions of disrespect quietly erode trust over time, why withdrawal during conflict amplifies tension rather than reducing it, and how to repair and redirect after a conversation goes badly.

- Advanced Conflict Skills (3-4 months): Follows stabilization. Covers why empathy resolves what logic cannot, how to debrief a fight after it happens to prevent resentment from calcifying, the difference between trying to win an argument and trying to understand one, how a partner's emotional responses either open or close connection over time, why refusing to be influenced by a partner reads as dismissal, how to recognize when a surface argument is actually about something deeper, and how to reach agreements both partners can live with without quiet resentment accumulating.

- Connection and Intimacy (3-4 months): Follows stabilization. Covers how to rebuild genuine affection and appreciation when daily frustration has replaced it, how to stay curious about who your partner is becoming rather than relating to who they were, how to recognize and respond to the small daily moments when a partner reaches for connection, why consistent small gestures build more intimacy than occasional grand ones, how to restore playfulness and fun when the relationship has become purely functional, how to support each other's individual dreams while building shared rituals and meaning, and how emotional reconnection is the precondition for restoring physical and sexual intimacy.

- Relationship Clarity (4-6 weeks): An alternative starting point for couples uncertain whether to continue the relationship. Covers the specific patterns research identifies as predictors of relationship success or failure, why certain conflicts keep returning and what that means for the relationship's future, how accumulated hurt distorts perception of a partner's intentions and behavior, and how to assess trust and commitment honestly when the answer is no longer clear. Couples who decide to continue can apply partial credit toward stabilization.
