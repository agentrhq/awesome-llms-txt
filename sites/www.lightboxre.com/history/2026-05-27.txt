## LightBox Connects Series
- [Episode 7: Should you include PFAS in your report](https://www.lightboxre.com/connects/episode-7-should-you-include-pfas-in-your-report/): In this episode of LightBox Connects, Victor DeTroy, National Practice Leader for AEI Consultants, discusses if, when and how you might need to include PFAS levels in a report\. Technically, PFAS are non\-scope and the rules on inclusion vary from state to state, although the Toxic Substances Act \(TSCA\) requires reporting of approximately 170 different PFAS\. So how wide do you cast your net regarding PFAS when you’re assessing a site? Hear Victor talk about how AEI uses a risk pyramid to help make that determination\.
- [Episode 6: E1527\-21: Risks of late adoption](https://www.lightboxre.com/connects/episode-6-e1527-21-risks-of-late-adoption/): In this episode of LightBox Connects, Dianne Crocker, Principal Analyst and Alan Agadoni, General Manager of LightBox talk about the industry's transition over to ASTM E1527\-21 with important advice for any environmental professionals who are still on the fence about transitioning over to the latest version of the Phase I ESA standard\.

- [Episode 5: Dissecting data challenges](https://www.lightboxre.com/connects/episode-5-dissecting-data-challenges/): Companies making the biggest strides in the CRE ecosystem have figured out how to leverage data to drive better decisions\. But using data effectively comes with its challenges\. In this episode, Patrick Jacolenne, Founder and Chief Data Strategy Officer at DATALOGIQ 360 explores major data obstacles including stale data and lack of consistency in the way the data is sourced, managed, and leveraged across various entities\. He’ll also explore the huge opportunities around standardizing proprietary data to drive your business forward\.
- [Episode 4: Uncovering cracks in investor confidence](https://www.lightboxre.com/connects/episode-4-principal-analyst-dianne-crocker/): In this episode, Dianne Crocker from LightBox sheds light on investor sentiment and cracks in the foundation of market confidence\. You’ll also get a deep dive into 2023 trends with promising outcomes for CRE including: Creativity and innovation, life sciences, robotics, and the sustainability race to net zero carbon\.
